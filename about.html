Our Story

Made slowly, by hand, with clay that remembers where it came from.

Clay & Fire Studio began as a single wheel in a converted garage and grew into a small workshop where every piece still passes through one set of hands from raw clay to finished glaze.

How it began

Founder Maren Voss started throwing pots after a decade in restaurant kitchens, chasing the same feeling good tableware gave her — food, and the people sharing it, made to feel a little more cared for. What began as weekend batches for friends became a full studio practice in Riverbend, Oregon.

Today the studio is still small on purpose. Two potters, one kiln, and a waitlist we're proud of — because rushing clay never ends well, and it never will here.

Our Approach

Every piece follows the same unhurried path — one that respects the clay's pace, not a production schedule.

01

Thrown by Hand

Each form starts as a centered lump of stoneware, shaped on the wheel with no molds and no shortcuts.

02

Slow Fired

Pieces rest for days before a gentle bisque, then a high stoneware firing that lets glazes settle into real depth.

03

Finished with Care

Every base is sanded smooth by hand and inspected before it ever reaches your table.

Our Commitment

We use locally sourced stoneware clay, lead-free glazes, and a kiln fired on renewable electricity whenever the grid allows. Nothing leaves the studio without our hands on it first.

  • Small batches — never mass produced
  • Food-safe, lead-free glazes on every piece
  • Imperfections left in, because handmade should look handmade
